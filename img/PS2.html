# Define the parameters of the model
alpha = 1/3
delta = 0.05
alpha_m = alpha/(1-alpha)

The Solow model's steady state income per worker ratio for country $i$ is equal to

$$ y_i = A_i^{1/(1-\alpha)}\left(\frac{\gamma_i}{n_i + \delta_i} \right)^{\alpha/(1-\alpha)}h_i $$

.

Then relative income per worker of country $i$ and country $j$ would be:

$$ \frac{y_i}{y_j} = \frac{A_i^{1/(1-\alpha)}\left(\frac{\gamma_i}{n_i + \delta_i} \right)^{\alpha/(1-\alpha)}h_i}{A_j^{1/(1-\alpha)}\left(\frac{\gamma_j}{n_j + \delta_j} \right)^{\alpha/(1-\alpha)}h_j} $$

If country $i$ and country $j$ differ only with respect to their investment rates:

$$\frac{y_i}{y_j} = \left( \frac{\gamma_i}{\gamma_j} \right)^{\alpha/(1-\alpha)} $$

4. Using the Penn World Table, conduct growth accounting of Italy, Greece, Spain, South Korea and the United Kingdom. Examine data from 1975 to 2014. Use rgdpna and rkna as output and capital stock measures. Create a table (similar to Table 7.3 of the textbook). Briefly comment on the table.

Solution: First, calculate growth rates per year from 1975 to 2014, $$ g_y = \left(\frac{y_{2014}}{y_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$ $$ g_k = \left(\frac{k_{2014}}{k_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$ $$ g_h = \left(\frac{h_{2014}}{h_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$

Assuming $y=A k^\alpha h^{1-\alpha}$, then $g_y = g_A + \alpha g_k + (1-\alpha)g_h$. Therefore, growth rate of productivity is $$g_A = g_y - \alpha g_k - (1-\alpha)g_h$$.

Lastly, contribution of productivity growth to income growth is equal to $g_A/g_y$, contribution of physical capital growth to income growth is $\alpha g_k/g_y$, and contribution of human capital growth to income growth is $(1-\alpha)g_h/g_y$

The variables we use are

rgdpna: Real GDP at constant 2011 national prices (in mil. 2011US\$)

rkna: Capital stock at constant 2011 national prices (in mil. 2011US\$)

a. Derive change in capital per effective worker, $\dot{\tilde{k}}$, as a function of capital per effective worker, $\tilde{k}$, and other exogenous variables in the model.

b. Find steady state capital per effective worker, income per effective worker, consumption per effective worker.

c. Suppose at time $t=0$, the economy is at the steady state, and level of labor-augmenting technology is equal to 1, $e(0)=1$. What's the income per worker level at time $t=20$? Remember that if a variable (say M) grows at a constant rate, say m, then the value of M at time $t$ is equal to $\exp(mt)$ times the value of M at time $0$, i.e. $M(t)=M(0)\exp(mt).$

In this question, the key observation you need to make is after the jump in labor augmenting technology, capital per effective worker goes down. To see this, remember the definition capital per effective worker, $\tilde{k} = \frac{K}{eL}$. At the instant of the shock, $K$ and $L$ are predetermined (or they don't change). However, $e$ goes up by 10 percent, hence $\tilde{k}$ goes down. $\tilde{k}^{new} = \frac{\tilde{k}}{1.1}$. After we made this observation, we can simulate our model starting from $\tilde{k}^{new}$.
